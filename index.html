How To Set Up A Lock Screen Passcode For Apple iPhone and iPad

  • First, in the home screen, head over to "Settings".
  • Then, scroll down and open "Touch ID & Passcode".
  • After that, tap "Turn Passcode On" or "Change Passcode".
  • Next, the device will prompt you to insert a series of number that will be utilized as your passcode. Keep in mind that occasionally, Apple may warn you that your passcode can be easily guessed. You can either modify or use it anyway.
  • Finally, type down the chosen password again to verify it.
  • Setting a pascode turns on data protection, which encrypts your device with 256-bit AES encryption.
  • After you set a passcode, you can use Touch ID to unlock iPhone. For additional security, however, you must always enter your passcode to unlock your iPhone when you turn on or restart your iPhone, when you haven't unlocked the iPhone for more than 48 hours, and when there are five unsuccessful attempts to unlock your iPhone with Touch ID.
